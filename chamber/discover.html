About

Biliran Island is a picturesque island province located in the Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines, officially established as a regular province on May 11, 1992, following a plebiscite under Republic Act No. 7160. Situated between Leyte and Samar Islands, it is bordered by the Visayas Sea to the north, Carigara Bay to the south, the Samar Sea to the east, and the Strait of Biliran to the west. The province is composed of eight municipalities: Almeria, Biliran, Cabucgayan, Caibiran, Culaba, Kawayan, Maripipi, and its capital, Naval. The province is renowned for its abundant natural attractions which draw both local and international tourists. The island's charm lies in its pristine environment, making it a growing destination for eco-tourism.
